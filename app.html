Investor Glossary

Everything you need to know to analyze a deal like a pro.

📊 Metrics 🏗️ Strategies 💰 Financing

Metric Cap Rate

Capitalization Rate. The annual return on a property based on its price — assuming you paid cash with no mortgage.

📐 Formula: (Annual Net Income ÷ Purchase Price) × 100
✅ 6%+ is decent. 8%+ is strong. 10%+ is excellent.

Metric Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC)

How much cash you actually earn per year relative to the cash you put in — including your mortgage payment.

📐 Formula: (Annual Cash Flow ÷ Total Cash Invested) × 100
✅ 8%+ is solid. 12%+ is great.

Metric DealScore™

DealScope's proprietary score from 0–100 that ranks a property based on 6 factors: undervaluation, cash flow, CoC, value-add upside, market momentum, and condition.

🏆 85+ = Grade A (top deals)
70–84 = Grade B (solid)
55–69 = Grade C (fair)

Metric Cash Flow

The money left over each month after collecting rent and paying all expenses: mortgage, taxes, insurance, repairs, and vacancy.

📐 Rent − Mortgage − Taxes − Insurance − CapEx = Cash Flow
✅ Positive = money in your pocket every month.

Metric ARV (After Repair Value)

What a property will be worth AFTER you fix it up. This drives your BRRRR refinance and flip profit calculations.

🏠 Example: Buy for $120k, spend $30k on rehab → ARV $180k = $30k in equity created.

Metric DOM (Days on Market)

How long the property has been listed. High DOM often means the seller is more motivated and open to lower offers.

📅 Under 30 days = competitive. 60+ days = negotiating power. 90+ = seller likely desperate.

Metric% Below Comp

How much below the neighborhood's comparable sold prices (comps) this property is listed. Higher = more undervalued.

✅ 5%+ below comps is worth a look. 10%+ is a serious discount. 15%+ is rare and valuable.

Metric Equity

The difference between what a property is worth and what's owed on it. High equity owners are more likely to sell at a discount.

🏠 Home worth $300k, mortgage $100k = $200k equity (67%).
High equity = motivated seller potential.

Strategy BRRRR

Buy · Rehab · Rent · Refinance · Repeat. Buy a distressed property, fix it up, rent it out, then refinance to pull your cash back out — and repeat.

🔄 The goal: get most or all of your cash back at refi so you can buy the next property. Use the BRRRR tab to model it.

Strategy Cash Flow

Buy and hold properties that generate positive monthly income after all expenses. The goal is passive monthly income.

💰 Best for: building long-term wealth and monthly income.
Look for: cap rate 6%+, CoC 8%+.

Strategy Fix & Flip

Buy a distressed property, renovate it, and sell it at a profit — usually within 6–12 months.

📐 MAO (Max Allowable Offer) = ARV × 70% − Rehab Costs
Example: $200k ARV × 70% − $30k rehab = $110k max offer.

Financing LTV (Loan-to-Value)

The percentage of a property's value that a lender will loan you. Most conventional lenders go up to 80% LTV on investment properties.

🏦 80% LTV on $200k = $160k loan. You bring $40k down.
BRRRR refi typically 75% LTV on ARV.

Financing Hard Money Loan

A short-term, high-interest loan from a private lender — used to buy and rehab properties quickly when banks won't lend.

⚡ Typical: 10–14% interest, 6–18 month term, quick approval.
Common for flips and BRRRR acquisitions.

Financing CapEx

Capital Expenditures — money set aside each month for big future expenses like roof replacement, HVAC, plumbing, etc.

💡 Rule of thumb: budget 5–10% of monthly rent for CapEx.
$1,500/mo rent → budget $75–$150/mo for CapEx.

Strategy 🏠 House Hacking

Buy a multi-unit property or a home with an ADU, live in one unit, and rent out the others. The rental income offsets — or even eliminates — your mortgage payment.

🔑 Why it's genius: You use owner-occupant financing (FHA, 3.5% down) instead of investor financing (20–25% down). Lower barrier, lower rate.

💡 Example: Buy a duplex for $165k. Live in Unit A. Rent Unit B for $1,200/mo. Your mortgage is ~$1,350/mo. You live there for $150/mo and build equity.

Strategy Skip Trace

The process of finding contact information (phone, email) for a property owner so you can reach out about buying their property directly — before it hits the market.

📞 Used by wholesalers and investors to find off-market deals.
Available on DealScope Pro.

Metric NOI (Net Operating Income)

Annual rental income minus all operating expenses (taxes, insurance, maintenance, management) — but before mortgage payments.

📐 NOI = Gross Rent − Vacancy − Operating Expenses
Cap Rate = NOI ÷ Purchase Price

🎯 How to Analyze Your First Deal

Step 1 — Filter for deals
Set Cap Rate to 6%+, DealScore to 75+. This gives you only the top properties worth looking at.
Step 2 — Check the numbers
Open a property and review the Investment Analysis section. Is cash flow positive? Is CoC above 8%?
Step 3 — Compare top picks
Add your top 2–3 to the Compare tab and look at them side by side before deciding.
Step 4 — Run the BRRRR
If it needs work, plug the numbers into the BRRRR tab to see how much cash you'd get back at refinance.
